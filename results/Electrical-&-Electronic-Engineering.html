General Electives
Unrestricted Electives in Electrical & Electronic Engineering 2014 2
COURSE CODE TITLE AU PROGRAMME/(DEPT MAINTAIN*)
EE8064 INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY FOR ELECTRONICS ENGINEERS 3.0 EEE
Introduction to Intellectual Property (IP) from the perspective of electronics engineering. Importance of IP rights for Integrated Circuits (ICs). Technical contexts of IC. Types of IP for IC protection. Ownership of IC IP rights. Infringement of IC IP rights. Commercial dealings. Procedure for filing for IP Protection.
 
EE8067 CERAMICS IN HISTORY,ARTS,GEMSTONES,ENVIRONMENT & MODERN LIFE 3.0 EEE
Prerequisite: O-level Physics or equivalent
Definition of ceramics and overview. Ceramics in History and Art. Fundamental Concepts. Jewelleries and Gemstones. Ceramics in Civil and Environment. Ceramics in Electrical and Electronic devices, Science, Technology and Applications of ceramics in modern life.
 
EE8084 CYBER SECURITY 3.0 EEE
Introduction to Cyber Crimes and Security Issues in a Cyber-environment. System Perspectives of Information Security: Issues and Solution Approaches. Concepts for Secrecy, Integrity and Availability. Security Solutions and Models. Security Planning & Management. Security Cases and Technology Trends.
 
EE8086 ASTRONOMY - STARS, GALAXIES AND COSMOLOGY 3.0 EEE
The origin of modern astronomy - an introduction, Learn to read the stars, Overview of the solar system, The beginning and life of stars, The mysteries ahead, The future of space exploration.
 
EE8087 LIVING WITH MATHEMATICS 3.0 EEE
Solving algebraic equations and applications. Trigonometry with applications. Conic sections: straight line, circle, hyperbola, parabola, ellipse. Planets of the universe. Functions in daily life. Applications of differentiation and integration. Personal finance.
 
EE8092 DIGITAL LIFESTYLE 3.0 EEE
Home Entertainment Systems and Game Consoles. Digital Audio Systems. Digital Cameras and Video Camcorders. Personal Computers. Mobile Phones and PDA.
 
EE8093 ENERGY DEVICES FOR SUSTAINABLE URBAN ENVIRONMENT 3.0 EEE
This course will introduce students to the basic operating principles and applications of photovoltaic devices used for solar energy conversion, thermoelectric devices for energy harvesting and electrochemical devices for renewable energy storage. Contents: Sustainability Concepts. Crystalline Solar Cells. Thin Film Solar Cells. Thermoelectric Devices. Supercapacitors. Batteries.
 
IC9206 WIRELESS NETWORKS 3.0 EEE
Mutually exclusive with: IC0206
Not available to Programme: BCE, BCG, BUS(IT), CE, CEEC, CSC, CSEC, EEE, EEEC, ENG, ENG(EEE), IEEC, IEM
Basic Concepts of Wireless Communications. Basic Cellular Concepts and Infrastructures. Wireless Local Area Networks. Wireless Network Protocols.

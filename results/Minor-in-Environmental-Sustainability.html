COURSE CODE TITLE AU PROGRAMME/(DEPT MAINTAIN*)
EM9101 ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY 3.0 CEE Mutually exclusive with: EM101, MT3302 Not available to Programme: CEE, CVEC, ENE, ENEC, ENG(CEE), ENG(ENE), MS(2004-2010), MS(MSB)(2004-2010) This is an introductory course on environmental engineering. Topics include ecological concepts, environmental engineering basics, and water, air, land and other environmental quality issues. HE3005 ENVIRONMENTAL ECONOMICS 3.0 ECON(HSS) Prerequisite: HE101 OR HE191 OR AB106 OR HU101 OR HE1001 OR HE9091 OR AB0901 OR HU9001 Mutually exclusive with: HE305 Not available to all Programme: (Admyr 2004-2010) This course covers the valuation of environmental quality, assessment of environmental damages. Analytical tools needed for the evaluation of projects which affects the environment such as cost-benefit analysis, and environment impact analysis will also be presented. Further topics will include aspects of sustainable development, the problems associated with natural resource management, and select topics on international environmental issues as in global warming and climate change, and transboundary pollution. HE3010 ENERGY ECONOMICS 3.0 ECON(HSS) Prerequisite: HE101 OR AB106 OR HE191 OR HU101 OR HE1001 OR HE9091 OR AB0901 OR HU9001 Mutually exclusive with: HE310 Not available to all Programme: (Admyr 2004-2010) The first half of this course covers the scientific and engineering nature and characteristics of energy, while the second part deals with the economics and consequence of using energy and its implications on achieving a sustainable economy. It also examines how the reality of energy resource uses diverges from the theory and reviews the structure of energy demand and supply in detail, and considers the perspectives on the possibility of infinitive supply of energy resources.

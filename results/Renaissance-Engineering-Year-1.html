RE1002 ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION ENGINEERING I 3.0 AU
The first part of this course provides an introduction to circuit analysis. The topics to be covered are basic circuit theorems, resistive networks, inductors, capacitors, energy storage and transient responses, alternating current circuits, complex impedance, Laplace transforms in circuit analysis, frequency response and filter design. This course then introduces basic electronic devices, such as diodes and their application in rectifiers, MOSFET, amplifier circuits, operational amplifiers and their applications. The second part of this course covers digital electronics, which includes number systems and logic gates, Boolean algebra, combinatorial logic circuits, flip-flops, sequential logic circuits, synchronous sequential logic circuits, programmable logic devices and memories.

RE1021 MATHEMATICS II 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: RE1001
Mathematics II serves as a second mathematics course for all Renaissance students in year 1. This course covers: Linear Algebra: Systems of linear equations, Gaussian elimination. Matrices, inverses, determinants. Vector spaces, subspaces, linear independence, basis, dimension, row and column spaces, rank, linear transformations, eigenvectors, eigenvalues, diagonalization. Inner products, inner product spaces, orthonormal sets, Gram-Schmidt process, Fourier series. Calculus of Several variables: Partial derivatives, limits and continuity, chain rule, directional derivatives, gradients, Lagrange multipliers. Double integrals, area of a surface, triple integrals. Vector calculus, line integrals, Green?s Theorem, surface integrals, Gauss?s divergence theorem, Stokes? Theorem. Through the sample questions/exercises provided in the course material, the students will have chances to pick up the fundamental principles for engineering science applications.

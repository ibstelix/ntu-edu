EM9101 ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY 3.0 CEE Mutually exclusive with: EM101, MT3302 Not available to Programme: CEE, CVEC, ENE, ENEC, ENG(CEE), ENG(ENE), MS(2004-2010), MS(MSB)(2004-2010) This is an introductory course on environmental engineering. EM9106 ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT 3.0 CEE Mutually exclusive with: EM106 Not available to Programme: ENE, ENEC, ENG(ENE) Environment systems; direct and indirect impacts; impact assessment and analysis; planning tools; assessment methodologies; water, air, noise, social and economic impacts and planning issues; management of impacts; assessment indices and reports; environmental legislations. HE3005 ENVIRONMENTAL ECONOMICS 3.0 ECON(HSS) Prerequisite: HE101 OR HE191 OR AB106 OR HU101 OR HE1001 OR HE9091 OR AB0901 OR HU9001 Mutually exclusive with: HE305 Not available to all Programme: (Admyr 2004-2010) This course covers the valuation of environmental quality, assessment of environmental damages. Analytical tools needed for the evaluation of projects which affects the environment such as cost-benefit analysis, and environment impact analysis will also be presented. Further topics will include aspects of sustainable development, the problems associated with natural resource management, and select topics on international environmental issues as in global warming and climate change, and transboundary pollution. HE3010 ENERGY ECONOMICS 3.0 ECON(HSS) Prerequisite: HE101 OR AB106 OR HE191 OR HU101 OR HE1001 OR HE9091 OR AB0901 OR HU9001 Mutually exclusive with: HE310 Not available to all Programme: (Admyr 2004-2010) The first half of this course covers the scientific and engineering nature and characteristics of energy, while the second part deals with the economics and consequence of using energy and its implications on achieving a sustainable economy. It also examines how the reality of energy resource uses diverges from the theory and reviews the structure of energy demand and supply in detail, and considers the perspectives on the possibility of infinitive supply of energy resources. HE305 ENVIRONMENTAL ECONOMICS 4.0 ECON(HSS) Prerequisite: HE9092 OR HE101 OR HE191 OR AB106 OR HE192 OR HU101 OR HU9001 OR AB0901 OR HE9091 OR HE1001 Mutually exclusive with: HE3005 Not available to all Programme: (Admyr 2011-2050) This course deals with the application of microeconomic tools to the analysis of environmental externalities. A main theme is the impact of economic activity on the quality of the environment and the related policies and regulations that arise in response to these impacts. The course will cover methods of valuing non-market environmental resources; assessment of environmental benefits and damages; analytical tools such as cost-benefit analysis needed for project evaluation and environment impact assessments; the concept of optimal pollution and its control; regulation and market solutions. Selected topics on international environmental issues and aspects of sustainable development will also be discussed. HE310 ENERGY ECONOMICS 4.0 ECON(HSS) Prerequisite: HE101 OR AB106 OR HE191 OR HU101 OR HE1001 OR HE9091 OR AB0901 OR HU9001 Mutually exclusive with: HE3010 Not available to all Programme: (Admyr 2011-2050) This course seeks to give students an introduction to the economics of energy use and its relationship with the environment. It looks at energy demand and supply, both now and in the future, and energy markets and trading (in particular, in oil markets). Alternative energy supply technologies and the economics of renewable resources are also examined. Issues such as monopoly power in electricity markets and regulation and deregulation in such markets will also be discussed. Finally, it deals with issues relating to energy security and the effect of energy use on the environment and climate change. HG2020 LANGUAGE IN SOCIETY 3.0 LMS(HSS) Prerequisite: HG1002 OR HU9001 OR HG102 OR HU101 Mutually exclusive with: HG220 Not available to all Programme: (Admyr 2004-2010) Topics covered include: approaches to the study of language in its social context; linguistic diversity and societal multilingualism; language planning and policy (with special focus on Singapore); the role of language in social stereotypes and identity; social norms and language choice; speech communities and social networks; the effect of social factors on language variation; and the linguistic consequences of language contact, with particular emphasis on contact languages (pidgins and creoles) and codeswitching. HS3004 CITIES AND URBAN LIFE 3.0 SOC(HSS) Prerequisite: HS101 & HS202 & HS203 OR HS101 & HS202 & HS204 OR HS101 & HS202 & HS205 OR HS101 & HS203 & HS204 OR HS101 & HS203 & HS205 OR HS101 & HS204 & HS205 OR HS101 & HS201 & HS202 OR HS101 & HS201 & HS203 OR HS101 & HS201 & HS204 OR HS101 & HS201 & HS205 OR HS1001 & HS2001 & HS2002 OR HS1001 & HS2001 & HS2002 OR HS1001 & HS2001 & HS2003 OR HS1001 & HS2001 & HS2004 OR HS1001 & HS2001 & HS2005 OR HS1001 & HS2002 & HS2003 OR HS1001 & HS2002 & HS2004 OR HS1001 & HS2002 & HS2005 OR HS1001 & HS2003 & HS2004 OR HS1001 & HS2003 & HS2005 OR HS1001 & HS2004 & HS2005 Not available to all Programme: (Admyr 2004-2010) This subject examines theories of urban development and features of urbanism as a way of life, focusing on processes of urbanisation and metropolitan development in both the developed world and in the developing world. It considers the urban transformation of predominantly rural societies, highlighting the implications of the rural-urban divide and issues related to urban poverty, housing and urban renewal. The subject also discusses the rise of global cities and informational cities--and the rise of the creative city--with emphasis on the competition between cities in attracting trade, talent and tourists and the potential collaboration between them in addressing problems engendered by the global economy and international migration.

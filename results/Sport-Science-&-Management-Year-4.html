SS3102 EXERCISE & SPORT PHYSIOLOGY III 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: ABA328 OR
SS1006 & SS3101 OR
ABA106 & SS3101
A lecture/laboratory course is designed to provide a basic framework that will aid the third year students in acquiring knowledge and technical laboratory skills pertinent to exercise physiology. The laboratory techniques and knowledge will help students to understand the demands of training on the body and will enable them to prescribe exercise or training for optimal health and elite performance. Emphasis will be placed on the energy contribution during high intensity exercise, prediction of performance in endurance events, lactate threshold/ventilatory thresholds and maximal oxygen uptake. The importance of body composition and the different assessment modes will also be emphasised.

SS3202 SPORT BIOMECHANICS III 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: SS2003 & SS3201 OR
ABA325
This course will focus on the quantitative analysis of human movement. Participants will learn how to design a quantitative analysis, collect, analyse and interpret data obtained from the equipment associated with the measurement technique. From the analysis work of this course, participants will be expected to examine the relationship between performance measure and human motor system. Participants will have the opportunity to explore the underlying processes that is involved in the production of this human movement. The course will consist of theory and applied field work.

This course includes an overview of varied psychology topics (e.g., individual differences/personality, social influence and group dynamics, diversity issues, and applied psychological skills). SS4402 LEADERSHIP & INSTRUCTION IN PHYSICAL ACTIVITY & SPORT 3.0 AU
This course is characterised by a myriad of complex processes involving strategic and analytic thinking, vision and detailed planning, and ongoing authentic creation of a physically literate learning community. At the heart of successful leadership is a passion for learning underpinned by a set of essential skills and qualities that enable the leader to perform their key leadership tasks in physical activity and sport. Those who succeed in physical activity and sport tend to be leaders who draw on their personal values and abilities to maintain and develop a culture of learning and achievement, whilst simultaneously managing the ongoing tensions and dilemmas that emerge in any physical activity or sport program. Leaders can exhibit a range of leadership styles, which become successful when they reflect the needs of their setting combined with their own deep motivations. It is essential that leaders of physical activity and sport understand the need to reflect on their own performance and engage in their own process of continuous learning and development.

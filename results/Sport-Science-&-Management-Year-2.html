SS0201 ETHICAL ISSUES IN SPORT 3.0 AU
Sport is a socially significant activity for many individuals. Moreover, the sports industry is global, saturates the media, influences young people and is a massive arm of the entertainment industry which generates billions of dollars each year. The influence that such a massive industry plays on society generates a unique set of ethical issues. This course will critically examine a number of the ethical issues surrounding the role of sport in society and ask the student to question and analyse the role of the sport in today?s world.
SS2001 EXERCISE & SPORT PSYCHOLOGY 1 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: ABA201
This focus of this module is on the social and psychological aspects of sport and exercise. Topics covered include personality, motivation, stress and coping, aggression, moral development, team building and cohesion, competition, and leadership. Emphasis will be on the role of sport psychology in influencing participants' cognition, emotion, and behaviour in physical activity and sport settings.
SS2006 RESEARCH METHODS & DESIGN IN PHYSICAL ACTIVITY & SPORT 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: ABA206
This course provides students with a foundation from which to critically examine quantitative and qualitative research designs and strategies. Every effort will be made to place such designs and strategies and their applications in basic or pure and applied settings of physical education and sport. Specifically, the module introduces the student to: the nature of the research process, the use of literature, the definition and evaluation of a research problem, hypothesis formation, scientific investigation and methods, types of research available and abstract and report writing.

SS9204 FLOORBALL 3.0 AU

SS9303 INTRODUCTION TO HIP-HOP AND ITS DANCES 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: Physically active.No injuries/medical conditions.
How many times have you wondered- what exactly is Hip-Hop? Over this course, learners will be exposed to how the Hip-Hop culture we know of today, born in the mid-1970s in an impoverished section of New York City, found its way to the mass media and mainstream population and evolved into a multi-million dollar industry. Emphasis will be placed on learning about roots of Hip-Hop, particularly its dances. Participants will learn to dance to the rhythm of music and be introduced to some common moves popularized by prominent dance pioneers.

SS9402 INTRODUCTORY SWIMMING 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: No injuries/med cond. No previous skills in basic swimming
The course aims to provide NTU undergraduate students with the basic skills and knowledge required to learn how to swim. Participants will learn the correct techniques required to swim the four strokes (frontcrawl, backstroke, butterfly and breaststroke). Skills like threading water, plunges/starts and turns/somersaults will be taught. In addition, FINA rules relating to these strokes will be introduced.

CZ4065 DIGITAL FORENSICS 3.0 CSC Prerequisite: CZ4062 & CZ4063 This course introduces digital forensics at an elementary level. Overview of forensic science, steps from collecting data to preserving evidence, and a framework for digital forensic evidence collection and processing. Fundamentals of host forensics for Microsoft Windows. Fundamentals of host forensics for Unix derivatives using the Linux operating system as an example. Introduction to steganographic techniques for images, video, textual data, and audio as well as steganalytical techniques for selected media types and approaches to traitor tracing. A survey of non-standard storage mechanisms from retention characteristics to mobile and smart phones and vehicular systems as well as network-based search and storage mechanisms. CZ4002 VISUAL MEDIA CPMPRESSION AND PROCESSING 3.0 CSC(CE) Not available to all Programme: (Admyr 2004-2010) This course introduces media management and processing at a more advanced level. 1. Overview of image and video compression: digital image and video signals; color sampling patterns; compression basics; quality assessment; applications of digital image and video coding; standardization activities. 2. Entropy coding: Measure of information content, entropy, Shannon source coding theorem; entropy coding; Huffman coding; modified Huffman coding; arithmetic coding; Run-length coding. 3. Digital image coding techniques: Image statistics measure; image coder architecture; quantization; scalar quantization; uniform quantization; vector quantization; predictive coding; DPCM; transform coding; discrete cosine transform; JPEG standard. 4. Digital video coding techniques: motion estimation; block-based motion estimation; exhaustive block matching; fast motion estimation; three step search; orthogonal search, hybrid video coding; intra frame and inter frame. 5. Image and video representation: global visual features, local visual features, biologically inspired visual features, interest point detection, and spatial-temporal data representation. 6. Image and video retrieval: similarity and dissimilarity measures, introduction to dimensionality reduction algorithms, large-scale image/video database organization, reranking, and retrieval representation. 7. Hypermedia: multimedia, hypertext, hypermedia and their applications.

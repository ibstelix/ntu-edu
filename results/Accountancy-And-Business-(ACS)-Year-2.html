BA2201 ACTUARIAL ECONOMICS 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: AB106 OR
AB0901
Mutually exclusive with: BA220
Not available to Programme: ACBS 3, ACBS(ACS) 3, BUS(ACS) 3
The course is an introduction to fundamental concepts of economics with an emphasis on the practical application of the concepts in a business environment, particularly in the context of insurers and financial institutions and their customers.
 
BA2204 MODELS 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: AB103 OR
AB1202
Mutually exclusive with: BA211
Not available to Programme: ACBS 3, ACBS 4, ACBS(ACS) 3, ACBS(ACS) 4, BUS(ACS) 3
The course aims to teach students about the fundamental principles and applications of survival models and how to model the behaviour of stochastic processes. Topics covered include introduction to stochastic process, Markov chains and its applications, multiple state models, Markov jump process, survival models, life tables, Kaplan-Meier and Nelson-Aalen estimators, Cox model, binomial and Poisson models, graduation, and statistical tests.
 
BA3202 ACTUARIAL STATISTICS 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: AB103 & BA215 OR
MAS315 & MAS317 OR
AB1202 & BA215 OR
MTH351 & MTH353 OR
AB1202 & BA2203 OR
AB103 & BA2203
Mutually exclusive with: BA322
Not available to Programme: ACBS 2, ACBS 4, ACBS(ACS) 2, ACBS(ACS) 4, BUS(ACS) 2
This course covers the application of probability theory and statistical methods to some insurance problems. The scope of the course more or less matches Subject CT6 - Statistical Methods of the Institute of Actuaries, UK, examinations. Topics covered include Decision Theory, Bayesian Methods, Loss Distributions, Risk Theory, Ruin theory, Reinsurance, Generalized Linear Models, Time Series Models, Simulation Methods and Experience Rating.
